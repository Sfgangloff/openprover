Define the function $T: \mathbb{N} \to \mathbb{N}$ by:

$$T(n) = \begin{cases} n/2 & \text{if } n \text{ is even} \\ 3n+1 & \text{if } n \text{ is odd} \end{cases}$$

Prove that for every positive integer $n$, there exists $k$ such that $T^{(k)}(n) = 1$, where $T^{(k)}$ denotes the $k$-th iterate of $T$.
